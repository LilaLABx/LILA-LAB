# BENI v1.0 Data Licensing and Use Directions

BENI v1.0 uses a layered rights model.

## BENI-Derived Materials

The following BENI-created or BENI-derived materials are released under the
Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International License (CC BY 4.0):

- article identifiers;
- publication dates and year-month fields;
- source provenance fields;
- harmonised category fields;
- duplicate flags and duplicate group identifiers;
- text hashes;
- economic seed labels;
- summary statistics;
- index-ready aggregate outputs;
- dataset documentation;
- release manifests and schema documentation.

Users may share and adapt these materials, including for commercial use, if
they provide attribution.

Recommended attribution:

> Nabil, A. N. (2026). BENI v1.0: A Harmonised Bangla News Dataset for
> Economic Narrative Measurement.

Users must also cite the relevant upstream sources, including the Potrika Bangla
News Corpus and BNAD where applicable.

## Full Article Text

Full article bodies and cleaned article text are not re-licensed by BENI unless
the relevant upstream source permits redistribution.

For any source where full-text redistribution rights are unclear, public BENI
releases should provide metadata, hashes, and reconstruction scripts rather than
redistributing article text.

## Code

BENI processing code should be released under the MIT License. See
`CODE_LICENSE.md`.

## User Responsibilities

Users are responsible for:

- respecting upstream corpus and news-source licences;
- citing BENI v1.0 and upstream datasets;
- not describing BENI as the original creator of the underlying news articles;
- checking whether their use of full article text is permitted by upstream terms;
- reporting source-break diagnostics when using the unified 2014-2024 panel.
