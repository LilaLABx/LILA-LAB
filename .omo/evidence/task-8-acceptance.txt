Each pipeline is named by taking the first letter of the target language and appending "ENI" (Exploration & Native-language Intelligence). Examples: BENI (Bangla), AENI (Assamese), NENI (Nepali), SENI (Sylheti), CENI (Chittagonian). Every XENI pipeline follows the same structure: news collection, LLM annotation, classifier training, index construction, and macroeconomic validation. technical-reports/paper1_systematic_review/beni_arxiv_final.tex:We conduct a three-part empirical study on sentiment-based economic forecasting: (1) a systematic review of 66 papers (2007--2025) documenting methodological evolution and geographic gaps; (2) a reproducible replication of 8 papers across dictionary, ML, and transformer methods, achieving a pooled median RMSE improvement of 3.76\% (range: \(-6.68\%\) to \(+31.5\%\)); and (3) BENI, the first Bangla economic sentiment index, with a novel time-series domain dynamics model. Key findings: median reported RMSE improvement in the literature is 20\%, but corrected for publication bias and weak baselines, the estimated true effect is 10--15\%. Publication bias is severe: 88\% of empirical papers report positive results versus an expected 50--60\% under the null. Geographic bias is extreme: 56\% of studies focus on the US, 84\% on English, and zero sentiment indices exist for Bangla-speaking regions (265M speakers). BENI achieves 89.4\% topic classification accuracy (macro F1 = 0.860) and introduces time-series domain coupling analysis (monetary--inflation: \(\rho = 0.80\), 95\% CI: [0.65, 0.89]), revealing macroeconomic transmission mechanisms visible in news narratives. We provide full reproducibility code and a validation framework, enabling researchers to build sentiment indices for underrepresented languages and regions.

technical-reports/paper1_systematic_review/beni_arxiv_final.tex:Each replication was run with fixed random seeds (numpy=42, sklearn=42, torch=42), identical train/test splits, and deterministic preprocessing. Temporal splits ensure no look-ahead bias (train \(\leq\) 2018, val \(\leq\) 2019, test \(>\) 2019 for time-series methods; fixed sample splits for synthetic data). Code and results are fully open-source.
technical-reports/paper1_systematic_review/beni_arxiv_final.tex:Temporal splitting: articles with \texttt{publication\_date \(\leq\) 2018-12-31} \(\rightarrow\) train (70,477), \texttt{2019-01-01 to 2019-12-31} \(\rightarrow\) validation (21,242), \texttt{\(\geq\) 2020-01-01} \(\rightarrow\) test (28,988). This prevents look-ahead bias in the time-series evaluation.
