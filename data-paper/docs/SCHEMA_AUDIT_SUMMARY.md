## Main Schema Difference

Potrika raw schema:

- CSV.
- `News` is article body.
- `Heading` is title.
- `Date` is publication date.
- `Source` is newspaper.
- `Category` is broad topic.

BNAD raw schema:

- JSONL.
- `Content` is article body.
- `Title` is title.
- `Time` is publication date/time text.
- `Category` is source-specific topic.
- `Tags` and `Meta` are optional.
- Newspaper/source comes from filename.

## Merge Implication

The merge is feasible, but the unified file must preserve:

- `dataset_source`: `potrika` or `bnad`.
- `source_file`.
- `newspaper`.
- `category_original`.
- `category_harmonised`.

Do not pool them blindly. Treat Potrika as the stable 2014-2020 baseline and BNAD as the post-2020 extension.
